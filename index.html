Portfolio

The Mothership's Fuselage Structure

Along with other parts and assemblies on the two UAVs built during my senior design project, I was responsable for the design of the light-ply structure shown. The structure needed to be light while retaining strength and having ample room to mount all the electronic components.

Senior Design Mothership

Led a four person team in the iterative design of the ten pound UAV shown. Once fabricated this UAV, nicknamed the "Mothership", will be capable of flying 7 miles from its launch site, deploy two smaller UAVs, and return to the launch site for recovery.

Senior Design Daughtership

Led a four person team in the iterative design of the one pound UAV shown. Once fabricated this UAV will be deployed from the "Mothership" UAV featured above, and will fly up to 8 miles to its final destination to deliver a one ounce payload.

Autonomous Robotics Club Robot

Developed a robot design for the Autonomous Robotics Club to meet all the requirements to compete in the IGVC Compeition. This robot was designed to use off the shelf parts to keep costs low and the need of any manufacturing experience to a minimum. I led several design reviews and discussions with the client to ensure the robot design was to their satisfaction.

3D Printable Swerve Robot

Designed and fabricated a 3D printable swerve drive robot to get a better understanding of the design complexity and controllability of such a drive system. The outcome of this project was an in depth knowledge of the mechanical constraints of an individually controlled wheeled drive train, as well as experience creating algorithms to control the complex drive system.

FRC Robot

I have spent 4 years designing, prototyping, and fabricating robots such as the one shown on the right. Throughout this time I have been both a team player and a team leader and have a great deal of experience designing and fabricating complex robotic mechanisms. While designing and fabricating these mechanisms I have needed to work within design constraints as well as safety and competition rules.

Gazebo Robot Simulation

As a founding member of the Autonomous Robotics Club at Iowa State University, I have helped pioneer the clubs code testing methods. I along with a few others on the team developed a Gazebo simulation of our robot to run untested autonomous code as an intermediary while the final robot is being built. This simulation will also serve as a safety procedure to test code before deploying it onto a live robot.

DIY 3-axis Joystick Controller

Created a 3-axis joystick to act as a universal controller for my personal robotic projects. This design uses off the shelf parts and is controlled using an arduino microprocessor.
